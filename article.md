### The Rising Power Demands of Frontier AI Models
Artificial intelligence is advancing at a breathtaking pace. But behind
every leap in capability lies an equally staggering growth in the
resources required to train these models. The chart below, from Epoch
AI, tracks one of the most striking trends in modern AI: the exponential
rise in power consumption needed to train frontier models.


### Power Use Doubles Every Year and a Half
The chart plots the total power draw required (in watts) for AI models
over time. The pink trend line shows that the power required for
training frontier models has been growing at roughly 2.1x per year.

This means that what required 1 megawatt in 2020 could require more than
10 megawatts just five years later. Training AI is no longer just about
algorithms and GPUs --- it's increasingly about electricity.

### Landmark Models on the Curve
Several notable models stand out on the chart:

- **GPT-3 (2020)** already required a massive jump in power compared to
  its predecessors.
- **GPT-4 (2023)** pushed the curve higher still, solidifying the link
  between scale and capability.
- **Llama 3 (2024)** and **Grok-3 (2025)** show the continuation of
  this trend, each sitting near or above the frontier line.

These dots represent the cutting edge of what AI can do, and they
highlight how expensive it has become to reach the frontier.

### The Energy Implications
The rise in training power requirements has three major implications:

1.  [**Infrastructure strain** --- Data centers hosting frontier model
    training runs need access to enormous amounts of electricity, often
    measured in tens or hundreds of megawatts.]
2.  [**Geopolitical importance** --- Access to cheap, reliable energy
    may determine where the world's most advanced AI is trained. Regions
    with abundant renewable power or nuclear capacity could become AI
    hubs.]
3.  [**Sustainability concerns** --- As the AI sector grows, questions
    about carbon impact will intensify. Balancing progress with
    sustainability will become a defining challenge.]

### Beyond Power: Efficiency vs. Scale
It's worth noting that while training requirements are growing steeply,
researchers are also making efficiency gains. Techniques like better
architectures, sparsity, quantization, and hardware advances can reduce
the watts-per-parameter ratio. But so far, those improvements are not
enough to offset the exponential growth in overall demand.

### So what?
The future of frontier AI is inseparable from its energy footprint.
Training today's most advanced models requires electricity on the scale
of small towns, and the trend shows no signs of slowing down.

If the trajectory holds, the next generation of models will not only
test the limits of compute but also the limits of our energy systems.
The question is no longer can we train larger models, but where will we
find the power to do it?
